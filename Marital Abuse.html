What is Marital/Relationship Abuse?

Domestic violence, spousal abuse, battering, or intimate partner violence, is the victimization of an individual with whom the abuser has an intimate or romantic relationship. The CDC defines domestic violence as "physical violence, sexual violence, stalking, and psychological aggression (including coercive acts) by a current or former intimate partner."
Types of abuse can include stalking, economical, emotional, sexual, physical, or neglect.

Facts you should know

  • 1 in 4 women and 1 in 7 men experience physical abuse at some point in their lifetimes
  • 1 in 3 women and 1 in 6 men experience sexual abuse at some point in their lifetimes
  • 1 in 6 women and in 19 men have experienced stalking in their lifetimes, usually by someone that they know.
  • Over 5 million acts of domestic violence occur anually to women 18 and with over with over 3 million occuring to men

Always be on the lookout for domestic abuse

Domestic abuse comes in many forms. Here are some things to look out for.

Warning Signs

  • Gaslighting - making you believe you're not reacting appropriately or misunderstanding a situation.
  • Belittement - shaming you and enforcing the idea that you are worthless.
  • Physical abuse - Hitting, witholding food, threatening violence with weapons
  • Controlling - Constant pressure over making you stay inside or always monitoring where you're going.
